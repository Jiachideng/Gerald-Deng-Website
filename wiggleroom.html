$68,000 Soundproof Booth Project



The idea for my soundproof booth project was born out of two personal experiences that highlighted a critical gap in our campus infrastructure. Two individuals close to me faced life-changing opportunities—a high-stakes finance interview and a crucial exam—only to have them disrupted due to the lack of quiet, private spaces on campus. Brandeis, despite having a business school, lacked dedicated booths for calls, meetings, or focused work.

Determined to solve this issue, I pitched the idea of bringing soundproof booths to campus and was awarded $68,000 in funding for the initiative, a project I have been working on since March 2024. Over the past months, I’ve collaborated with architects, campus planning teams, engagement directors, the financial student budgeting director, and the head librarian. Navigating challenges such as fire marshal regulations, electrical constraints, and building codes, I ensured the project remained within budget while meeting all safety and operational requirements.

Through these efforts, I successfully purchased seven booths and partnered with KI Furniture to bring them to life, ensuring students have access to the private, distraction-free spaces they need for academic and professional success.
